# T-Side Default for de_overpass

#### Introduction
Overpass is slightly CT-sided at pro level (~52–53% CT win rate) and the most utility-intensive map for T-side. Our default is built around a 4:1 Monster execute — 4 players hit B through Monster while 1 lurks Long A — which is the most common pro T-side pattern on this map. Monster (B Short tube) is the primary T entry to B Site, and a Toilets smoke plus B Heaven smoke are the two non-negotiable lineups every round.

#### Opening Utility Usage (0-5s)
▸ [Support 1]: Throw Toilets smoke from T Spawn staircase (stand at right railing of stairs up to Fountain, aim at specific wall mark, jump-throw) — non-negotiable first smoke; Fountain approach without Toilets smoked results in an instant CT Toilets pick ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [Support 2]: Throw B Heaven smoke from B Short / Waters area (stand behind B sign in B Short, aim and jump-throw) — mandatory for any B execute; CT standing Heaven kills all players exiting Monster ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [AWPer/Entry]: Flash Connector from T Spawn to support safe early Connector push and deny CT Short vision before B Short approach ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [IGL]: Molly B Main Monster corner or Fountain position to flush any aggressive CT holding those areas before the B 4:1 push begins.

#### Info-Gathering Positions
▸ [Entry]: Push B Short → Monster after Toilets smoke and B Heaven smoke are established — first player into Monster, calls CT B Site positioning.
▸ [Support 1]: Second body in B Short — provides pop-flash before Monster entry; holds B Short depth if CT wins the opening duel.
▸ [AWPer]: Hold B Short / Waters after Connector flash — takes aggressive peek toward CT from B Connector or B Short; can redirect to Fountain / A Long based on IGL information.
▸ [IGL]: Take Fountain / A Long — reads CT A Long positioning from Fountain; decides between B Monster commit or A execute based on CT numbers.
▸ [Lurker]: Hold Long A (from Fountain → A Long) — classic 4:1 setup; lurker shows presence on Long A with footstep sounds to prevent CT from committing all 5 toward Monster.

#### Default Split Routes
▸ Default 4:1 B Monster Execute (most common pro pattern): 4 players hit Monster with Heaven smoke (B Short) + Connector smoke + Monster pop-flash. Lurker holds Long A and makes noise to occupy CT attention. Entry exits Monster first; Support follows 0.5s behind. Plant is at Monster exit CT-side. CT cannot safely commit all 5 to B with a Long A lurk active.
▸ Default A Execute (3 A + 2 B distraction): 3 players push Fountain → A Long with Toilets (already up) + Bank + Trash smokes. 2 players hold B Short as a distraction to delay CT B-to-A rotation. Bank smoke is the first A-site smoke deployed — blocks the strongest CT anchor position on A.

#### Timing Breakpoints
▸ First 30s: Toilets smoke and B Heaven smoke thrown immediately. Entry + Support advance B Short. AWPer holds B Connector for info. IGL reads Fountain / A Long. Lurker shows Long A presence with movement sounds. Do NOT burn Connector smoke before the execute is confirmed — it is needed for B retake prevention.
▸ Mid-Round (30s-45s): If CT numbers on B are low (Entry reports passive B or no Heaven presence) — commit 4-man Monster execute with Connector smoke + pop-flash. If IGL reports A Long is passive — execute A with Bank + Trash smokes and 3 players through Fountain.
▸ Late Round (45s+): AWPer attempts a lone B Connector peek or a last-second Monster push. If Lurker has Long A control — hit A late with whatever utility remains. Bank smoke is mandatory for A — never enter A site without it.

#### Adapting to CT Reads
▸ If CTs are aggressive in Connector (early Short rush or CT Connector peek):
- Support throws a Connector smoke to seal it. AWPer holds from T side of Connector.
- Redirect 3 players to B Main with full utility once Connector is sealed — CT aggression toward Connector almost always leaves B Short shorthanded.
▸ If CTs are stacking B with 3+ players (all committed Monster-side):
- AWPer takes free Connector and A Short picks with A undefended.
- Execute A with Bank + Trash smokes and Entry from A Short under flash support. Lurker from Long A splits site from second angle.
▸ If CTs fast rush B Short (pushing into Monster before T entry):
- Entry retreats; both B Short players hold at Monster entrance.
- IGL calls redirect to A with full 3-man execute while B Short CTs are over-extended away from site.

#### Key Smoke Lineups (Pro Reference)
▸ Toilets smoke: T Spawn staircase right railing — aim at specific wall mark above Toilets archway — jump-throw. Allows Fountain approach and B Short advance without Toilets CT pick.
▸ B Heaven smoke: Behind B sign in B Short (Waters area) — jump-throw — completely blocks CT Heaven angle over Monster. Non-negotiable for B execute.
▸ Connector smoke (B execute): B Short / Monster approach — aimed at Connector passage — blocks CT mid player from rotating to B retake after plant.
▸ Bank smoke (A execute): Top Connector bench left side — normal throw — lands on Bank position (CT A site anchor); first A-site smoke deployed in any A execute.
▸ Trash smoke (A execute): A Long approach / A Main — aimed at Trash / short CT A wall — blocks CT from holding the Trash angle during A entry.
