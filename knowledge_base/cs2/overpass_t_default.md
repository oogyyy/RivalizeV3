# T-Side Default for de_overpass

#### Introduction
Our T-side default on Overpass focuses on Connector / A Short control for early information and simultaneous B Main pressure through Monster. Winning Connector gives us a dominant AWP position and forces CTs to defend across a wide rotational distance between A and B.

#### Opening Utility Usage (0-5s)
▸ [Support 1]: Throw a Playground smoke from T Spawn to block CT Playground position and allow safe B Main entry without being picked from that elevated angle ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [Support 2]: Throw a Fountain smoke from B Main to deny CT Fountain aggression and open B Main for safe early movement ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [AWPer/Entry]: Flash Connector from T Spawn to allow safe early Connector push and deny CT Short control ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [IGL]: Molly B Main Monster corner or Fountain position to flush any aggressive CT holding that area ▶ Watch lineup.

#### Info-Gathering Positions
▸ [Lurker]: Take the Connector route toward A Short to contest the early Connector duel and gather A Site CT positioning information.
▸ [AWPer]: Push Connector aggressively for an early pick, then hold from A Short if the duel is won.
▸ [Support 1]: Advance into B Main alongside Entry, applying consistent B-side pressure throughout the default phase.
▸ [Entry + Support 2]: Push B Main and call B commitment level once reaching the Fountain / Monster chokepoint area.
▸ [IGL]: Read both sides simultaneously — AWPer's Connector report and Entry's B report — before calling the site commit.

#### Default Split Routes
▸ Default B Execute: Entry + two Supports execute B with Playground + Fountain + Bank smokes. AWPer holds A Short / Connector to delay CT rotation. Lurker from Connector cuts A-to-B CT rotation via Short.
▸ Default A Connector Split: AWPer + Lurker push Connector / A Short. Entry follows. Smokes cover Back of A Site and the CT corner. Supports apply B Main threat as a distraction to delay CT rotation to A.

#### Timing Breakpoints
▸ First 30s: Contest the Connector duel and apply B Main pressure simultaneously. Identify CT numbers on both sides from the information gathered.
▸ Mid-Round (30s-45s): If Connector is won, decide between A Short execute (fast) or redirect 3 players to B. If B is clearly lighter, commit B.
▸ Late Round (45s+): If no clean commit is ready, slow down and look for a lone B execute or a last-second Connector push with the AWPer leading.

#### Adapting to CT Reads
▸ If CTs are aggressive in Connector (early Short rush or CT peek):
- Support drops a Connector smoke to seal it. AWPer holds from T side of Connector.
- Redirect 3 players to B Main with full utility once Connector is effectively sealed off.
▸ If CTs are stacking B with 3+ players:
- AWPer takes free Connector and A Short picks.
- Execute A Short with Back + CT smokes and Entry from Short under flash support.
