# CT-Side Default for de_nuke

#### Introduction
Nuke is the most CT-favored map in CS2 (~57% CT win rate) because of vertical map control and fast internal rotations via Vents. CTs must hold Upper Site, Outside/Yard, and Ramp simultaneously, using the Vent system to shift between floors in seconds. Core principle: deny T Outside control with a Ramp molotov at round start every round, maintain the Heaven AWP, and never let the Outside player die without information coming first.

#### Opening Utility Usage (0-5s)
▸ [Ramp/Lobby Anchor]: Throw Ramp molotov from CT Lobby or Radio area landing at the bottom of Ramp — stops any T rushing Ramp in the first 5 seconds; mandatory every round without exception ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [Outside Player]: Pop Outside flash from CT side or Heaven Catwalk to blind Ts crossing from Red Container toward Garage or Secret — gives Outside CT player early information ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [AWPer]: Flash from Heaven position toward Outside immediately — takes an aggressive early peek from Heaven Catwalk angle to catch Ts crossing yard before they reach Garage cover ▶ Watch lineup.

#### Default Positions
▸ [AWPer]: Play Heaven / Rafters (Upper Site) — the most critical single position on the map; watches the entire Upper Site, Hut approach, Main stairs, and Heaven walkway simultaneously; covers Ramp from above.
▸ [Upper Anchor]: Hold Control Room / Under Heaven — watches Squeaky entry and Main corridor to Upper; second line of Upper defense behind AWPer.
▸ [Ramp/Lobby Anchor]: Play Ramp Room or Radio — holds T-side Ramp entry and Lobby cross; after throwing Ramp molotov, retreats to Radio to wait for molotov to expire.
▸ [Outside Player]: Play Garage or Heaven Catwalk — earliest information gatherer on the map; most exposed CT; must peek Yard quickly in the first 3 seconds and call T numbers before retreating.
▸ [IGL]: Hold Lower Site / Decon area — watches Silo approach and Lower Ramp exit; coordinates Upper/Lower rotation based on Outside player's report and Squeaky door audio.

#### Rotation Rules
▸ If Outside player dies (biggest information loss): remaining 4 assume T full-map control; Ramp player tightens to Lobby; IGL calls a pickup rotation and holds passively.
▸ If Squeaky door sound heard: Upper-site players close in; Ramp player positions to hold Squeaky; Heaven AWPer re-angles to watch Squeaky entry — Squeaky sound is the most reliable rotation trigger on the map.
▸ If Ramp smoke heard (Upper execute incoming): IGL in Lower drops into Decon to time a B-site save; Upper anchor throws molotov at Ramp bottom through Radio delay; AWPer stays in Heaven as long as possible.
▸ If Secret entry confirmed: Lower IGL alerts Upper immediately; Vent timing becomes critical — one CT drops via Vent to delay Lower B.
▸ If bomb planted Upper: Lower/Decon player climbs through Vent or takes Decon route — creates pincer retake with Heaven player descending via Ladder or Under Heaven.
▸ If bomb planted Lower: Upper players descend via Vent or Ramp; Outside player re-enters through Garage.

#### Crossfire Setups
▸ Heaven + Hut (Upper Site): AWP in Heaven watches Main entry and Squeaky from above; Hut player (or Under Heaven) watches from ground level. T pushing through Squeaky or up Main faces two elevation levels simultaneously — one angle is unreachable by a single flash.
▸ Radio + Ramp Room (Ramp): Radio player watches top of Ramp; Ramp Room player holds deeper. A T rushing Ramp must kill two separate angles along a single approach path.
▸ Garage + Heaven Catwalk (Outside): Two CTs form a pincer on Outside — one from Garage, one from Heaven Catwalk angle — any T passing through Yard faces two simultaneously unclearable angles.

#### Retake Coordination
▸ Upper Site (A) Retake: Vent player drops into A Site via Vent from Lower. Outside player enters through Garage → Main → Site. Flash from Main entry to blind Ts at Hut and Squeaky positions. Molotov standard A plant spot near Trophy / Central box. Vent entry and Main entry create a pincer — Ts cannot hold both angles. Vent arrival timing must be practiced precisely or it arrives too early (CT dies alone) or too late (bomb defused).
▸ Lower Site (B) Retake: Lower players enter from Decon door — throw flash into Lower before entry. Upper players drop via Vents simultaneously creating a two-level entry. Smoke the Silo window (Ts frequently hold Silo post-plant). Molotov the default Lower plant spot near Silo. The Vent drop bypasses all T utility set up for ground-level entries.

#### Adapting to T Reads
▸ If Ts are rushing Ramp with 4+ players:
- AWPer opens Heaven angle immediately — do not delay the first peek. Ramp player stays in Radio deep — NOT aggressive Ramp.
- Lower anchor stays Lower — do not pull for Upper unless T commitment to Upper is confirmed by sound.
▸ If Ts split Outside + Secret simultaneously:
- Do not rotate too early. Keep 2 Upper, 2 Lower, AWPer in Heaven until the primary threat is confirmed by both the Outside player's call and the Lurker's Secret sound.
- IGL calls which side is primary only after receiving both reports.
▸ If Ts run an Outside fake then commit Lower Secret:
- Ramp molotov already burned — CTs are not caught with Ramp utility. Hold Upper positions until Secret door is confirmed open.
- IGL in Lower drops to Decon immediately on Secret door audio.

#### Pro-Level Nuances
▸ Ramp molotov mandatory every round: Just like Banana on Inferno, Ramp gets molotov'd every round at pro level without exception. Skipping it gives Ts free Ramp in the first 5 seconds.
▸ Squeaky door audio is the most reliable Nuke trigger: Pro CTs can hear the Squeaky door from anywhere on the map — it is the single most important audio cue. Training to recognise it is essential.
▸ Vent timing is everything: Pro CTs practice exact Vent drop timing so they arrive in Lower/Upper right after a confirmed bomb plant. Too early = CT dies alone; too late = defuse already happening.
▸ Outside player is the round's information engine: If they die without calling T numbers, the whole CT economy of information collapses. Never send the Outside player to die without information.
▸ Lower Site gets 2 CT players: Amateur CTs neglect Lower completely. Pro CTs hold Lower with 2 players in default setups — leaving it with 1 risks an easy B win through Vents.
▸ Never let Ts walk Secret: Aggressive Outside CT + flash on Yard catches Ts before they reach the Secret staircase approach. Passive Outside = free Secret access every round.
