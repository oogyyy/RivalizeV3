# Pro Default Principles — CS2

These principles underpin every high-level competitive default across all maps and both sides. Apply them when generating any playbook section.

---

#### Principle 1: Information Before Commitment
A default is an information-gathering phase, not an execute. Never commit to a site without at least one concrete piece of information:
▸ A pick confirmed by the AWPer
▸ Sound cues (rotation footsteps, utility usage on a specific site)
▸ Visual confirmation at a key chokepoint

**Wrong:** Executing A at 1:20 because "we haven't heard anything."
**Right:** Executing A at 0:55 after AWPer gets a pick at Window, confirming CT is not playing aggressive mid.

---

#### Principle 2: Map Control Has Currency
Controlling a key map area forces CT rotations, creates dueling opportunities, and opens execute windows.
▸ Mid control → short-rotate capability to either site
▸ Flank route control → Lurker safety and CT rotation delay
▸ Close-range site control (Banana, Short) → reduces execute distance and entry angles

**Rule:** Win map control before attempting a split execute. A split with no map control usually results in catching T players in transit.

---

#### Principle 3: The Execute Window — 0:55 to 0:35
The optimal execute window is when the team has enough time to plant and survive a retake attempt (35–40 seconds post-plant), but not so early that CTs can retake with full utility remaining.
▸ 1:55 → Round starts
▸ 1:40 → Map control phase begins
▸ 1:00 → IGL should have a read forming
▸ 0:55 → Commit call / execute starts
▸ 0:40 → Bomb planted, post-plant phase
▸ 0:10 → Last defuse window; T must have all CTs eliminated

---

#### Principle 4: Utility Choreography — Timing Is Everything
A smoke thrown 3 seconds too early lands before entry. 3 seconds too late and the CT is already in position. Every utility item must have an assigned clock time.
▸ Pop flashes arrive 0.5s before entry — not before the smoke is up
▸ Site-clearing mollies go in as the last smoke is thrown, not before
▸ At least 2 smokes must be up simultaneously to execute; never rely on a single smoke
▸ Flashbang thrower must call "flashing [side]" on comms before throwing

---

#### Principle 5: Post-Plant Is a 5-Player Job
Winning the plant does not win the round. All 5 players must take pre-assigned positions.
▸ At least 1 player holds close (within 5 meters of bomb) to contest a defuse attempt
▸ At least 1 player watches the primary CT retake entry
▸ At least 1 player watches the secondary retake entry
▸ 1 player time-wastes or lurks to delay CT arrivals
▸ Never cluster — two players in the same spot gives CT team a 2-for-1 flash

---

#### Principle 6: CT-Side — Crossfires Over Lone Holds
A single CT holding a site is always at a disadvantage against a coordinated execute. Two CTs in a crossfire beat a 2-man entry trade.
▸ Players must be far enough apart that one flash cannot blind both
▸ One player holds the first contact angle; the other holds the trade angle
▸ Crossfire partners have a pre-agreed "break point" — if Player A dies, Player B immediately shifts to a new angle

---

#### Principle 7: Rotation Triggers Must Be Specific
"Rotate if you hear grenades" is too vague. Every rotation rule must name:
1. The specific sound or sight cue
2. Which player rotates
3. The exact route they take
4. What they call when arriving

**Wrong:** "AWPer rotates B if it sounds like B is getting hit."
**Right:** "If Lurker calls smoke + flash at B Main and confirms more than 2 Ts, AWPer drops Window and rotates through CT Spawn to Short, calling 'rotating B' on comms."

---

#### Principle 8: Economy Discipline
▸ If 2 or more teammates cannot full buy, coordinate to a uniform buy state — no one plays with a pistol while 3 teammates have rifles
▸ Save round means everyone saves — one player force-buying alone gifts Ts a free upgrade
▸ Never buy armor without a primary weapon
▸ The AWP is a team investment — AWPer plays conservatively when the team cannot replace a lost AWP

---

#### Principle 9: T-Side Calls Must Be Decisive
Mid-round calls must be made by 1:00 on the clock. If the IGL has not made a call by 0:55, the team defaults to the pre-agreed no-info execute (usually the site with the most utility coverage available).
▸ Teams should pre-agree at round start: "If we have no info by 0:55, we run [A/B execute]."
▸ This removes hesitation and decision paralysis late in rounds.

---

#### Principle 10: Adapting to Opponent Patterns
After 3–4 rounds, patterns become visible. Common adaptive reads:
▸ Opponent stacks A CT-side → default to B presses and B executes for the next 2–3 rounds
▸ Opponent runs fast rush B → play a 2-1-2 setup (2 on B, 1 mid, 2 on A) for 1 round
▸ Opponent plays aggressive mid → stack 2 players to clear mid before executing either site
▸ Opponent always rotates from CT → use a Lurker to cut off CT-side rotation and catch the rotating player
