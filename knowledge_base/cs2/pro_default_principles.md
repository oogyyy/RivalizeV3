# Pro Default Principles — CS2

These principles underpin every high-level competitive default across all maps and both sides. Apply them when generating any playbook section.

---

## 1. Information Before Commitment

A default is an information-gathering phase, not an execute. The team should **never commit to a site without at least one piece of concrete information**:
- A pick confirmed by the AWPer
- Sound cues (rotation footsteps, utility usage on a specific site)
- Visual confirmation at a key chokepoint

**Bad:** Executing A at 1:20 because "we haven't heard anything."
**Good:** Executing A at 0:55 after AWPer gets a pick at Window, confirming CT is not playing aggressive mid.

---

## 2. Map Control Has Currency

Controlling a key map area forces CT rotations, creates dueling opportunities, and opens execute windows.

| Map Control Won | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Mid control | Short-rotate capability to either site |
| Flank route control | Lurker safety; CT rotation delay |
| Close-range site control (e.g., Banana, Short) | Reduces execute distance and entry angles |

**Rule:** Win map control before attempting a split execute. A split with no map control usually results in catching T players in transit.

---

## 3. The Execute Window: 0:55 → 0:35

The optimal execute window is when:
- The team has enough time to plant and survive a retake attempt (~35–40 seconds post-plant)
- Not so early that CTs can retake with full utility remaining
- Not so late that the bomb cannot be planted and defended

**Clock reference:**
- 1:55 → Round starts
- 1:40 → Map control phase begins
- 1:00 → IGL should have a read forming
- 0:55 → Commit call / execute starts
- 0:40 → Bomb planted, post-plant phase
- 0:10 → Last defuse window; T must have all CTs eliminated

---

## 4. Utility Choreography: Timing Is Everything

A smoke thrown 3 seconds too early lands before entry; 3 seconds too late and the CT is already in position. Every utility item must have an **assigned clock time**.

**Principles:**
- Pop flashes arrive 0.5s before entry — not before the smoke is up
- Site-clearing mollies go in as the last smoke is thrown, not before
- At least 2 smokes must be up simultaneously to execute; never rely on a single smoke
- Flashbang thrower must call "flashing" so entry knows when to peek

---

## 5. Post-Plant Is a 5-Player Job

Winning the plant does not win the round. All 5 players must take **pre-assigned positions** based on the bomb's plant location.

**Rules:**
- At least 1 player holds close (within 5 meters of bomb) to contest a defuse attempt
- At least 1 player watches the primary CT retake entry
- At least 1 player watches the secondary retake entry
- 1 player time-wastes / lurks to delay CT arrivals
- Do not cluster — two players in the same position gives the CT team a 2-for-1 flash

---

## 6. CT-Side: Crossfires Over Lone Holds

A single CT holding a site is always at a disadvantage against a coordinated execute. **Two CTs in a crossfire beat a 2-man entry trade.** Crossfire principles:
- Players must be far enough apart that one flash cannot blind both
- One player holds the first contact angle; the other holds the trade angle
- Crossfire partners have a pre-agreed "break point" — if player A dies, player B immediately rotates to a different angle rather than holding the same exposed position

---

## 7. Rotation Triggers Must Be Specific

"Rotate if you hear grenades" is too vague. Every rotation rule must name:
1. The specific sound or sight cue
2. Which player rotates
3. The exact route they take
4. What they call when arriving

**Bad:** "AWPer rotates B if it sounds like B is getting hit."
**Good:** "If Lurker calls smoke + flash at B Main and confirms more than 2 Ts, AWPer drops Window and rotates through CT Spawn to Short, calling 'rotating B' on comms."

---

## 8. Economy Discipline

**Hard rules that never change:**
- If 2 or more teammates cannot full buy, the team drops to a coordinated buy (everyone buys similarly so no one has a stray AK that dies to an eco)
- Save round means everyone saves — one player force-buying alone gives Ts a free upgrade
- Never buy armor without a primary weapon
- The AWP is a team investment — the AWPer must play conservatively when the team cannot replace a lost AWP

---

## 9. T-Side Calls Must Be Decisive

Mid-round calls must be made by 1:00 on the clock. If the IGL has not made a call by 0:55, the team defaults to the pre-agreed "no-info" execute (usually the site with the most utility coverage available).

**No-info protocol:**
- Teams should pre-agree at round start: "If we have no info by 0:55, we run [A/B execute]."
- This removes hesitation and decision paralysis late in rounds.

---

## 10. Adaptations: Reading Opponent Patterns

After 3–4 rounds, patterns become visible. Common adaptive reads:
- **Opponent stacks A CT:** Default to B presses and B executes for the next 2–3 rounds
- **Opponent runs fast rush B:** Play a 2-1-2 setup (2 on B, 1 mid, 2 on A) for 1 round
- **Opponent plays aggressive mid:** Stack 2 players to clear mid before executing either site
- **Opponent always rotates from CT:** Use a Lurker to cut off CT-side rotation and catch the rotating player

---

## 11. Pistol Round Principles

**T pistol:**
- All 5 players should travel together or in coordinated sub-groups (3+2)
- Avoid splitting 5 ways with pistols — one site should be hit with at least 3 players
- At least 1 player buys armor; others buy HE grenades or a better pistol

**CT pistol:**
- Default CT pistol positions — no one plays aggressive AWP positions on pistol
- If the team has $1000 each, buy armor on at least 3 players (CTs with armor dominate pistol duels)
- At least 1 molotov or HE to delay T rush

---

## 12. Anti-Eco / Anti-Force Discipline

After winning a pistol round:
- Do NOT all buy rifles on the following eco round — the team's economy advantage comes from 2 or 3 bonus rounds
- At minimum, 3 players full buy; 2 players may save
- Play passive, let the eco team make desperate pushes, and trade efficiently
- One player with a scout or SMG is sufficient to anchor a passive hold
