# CT-Side Default for de_dust2

#### Introduction
Dust2 CT-side revolves around mid control and Long A information denial. Losing mid surrenders both B Short and Catwalk access simultaneously, forcing CTs to defend two sites without a clean rotation path. Core principle: AWP on Car every round, HE grenade into B Tunnels at round start, and contain B Tunnels with passive utility. CT win rate is ~50–51%, making this the most balanced map in the pool — execution and rotation discipline decide outcomes.

#### Opening Utility Usage (0-5s)
▸ [AWPer]: Wide-peek Long Doors from Car position in the first 3 seconds — catch Ts still running through Long Doors before they reach Bush or Blue; retreat to Car cover after the peek ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [B Anchor]: Throw HE grenade into Upper B Tunnels entry from B Site — chips 30–50 HP off rushing Ts and delays the B rush by 3–5 seconds; mandatory on every round ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [Mid Player]: Flash B Doors from Window / Scaffold position to catch early T Mid movement and deny free mid cross ▶ Watch lineup.

#### Default Positions
▸ [AWPer]: Play Car (Long A) — the single highest-value defensive position on the map; watches Long Doors, Blue, and A Short simultaneously; never abandon Car without a pick or confirmed Long clear.
▸ [Rifler 1]: Hold A Short / Pit — crossfire partner with AWPer on Long; watches the Long-to-A cross and Short-to-A approach.
▸ [Mid Player]: Hold CT Mid / B Doors / Window (Scaffold) — denies B Doors cross and Catwalk; a passive mid allows Ts to split both B (B Doors) and A (Catwalk) simultaneously.
▸ [B Anchor]: Hold Back Plat or B Window — passive deep B hold; watches Upper Tunnels exit and B site; does NOT chase Ts through Tunnels.
▸ [IGL]: Hold CT Spawn or B Doors area — reads T approach direction from B Tunnels sound and Long calls; first responder for rotation.

#### Rotation Rules
▸ If T AWP confirmed at Long (sound or visual): Rifler 1 assists AWPer from Pit / A Short; IGL holds B solo briefly; do not both leave B completely.
▸ If heavy B Tunnel movement heard (multiple footsteps): IGL + B anchor hold B passively (Back Plat); AWPer stays Long to deny A opening; mid player throws flash into Tunnels then drops to B Doors.
▸ If Catwalk push heard: Mid player flashes Catwalk and peeks to delay — calls it for A players to tighten on site.
▸ If Catwalk + Long smoked simultaneously (A execute): all CT players rotate A; B is temporarily abandoned.
▸ If B planted but only 1–2 Ts alive: AWPer rotates B via CT Spawn; Short rifler drops to B Doors; do not send both through Tunnels.

#### Crossfire Setups
▸ Car + Pit (Long A): AWP in Car holds Long Doors-to-open-ground; Pit player holds the left cross into A Site. T emerging from Long must face both simultaneously — cannot clear either without revealing themselves to the other.
▸ Back Plat + B Window (B Site): Back Plat player holds Upper Tunnels top exit (ground level); Window player holds B Site wide entry from above. A tunnels-rushing T must clear two very different vertical heights simultaneously.
▸ Short + CT Spawn (A Site access): Short player holds Catwalk-to-A angle; CT Spawn player covers the Long-to-A cross. Any T flanking from mid faces two contact points before reaching the site.

#### Retake Coordination
▸ A Site Retake: Short player flashes into site over the Short wall and enters first from Short. Long A player smokes Long exit or mollies the corner near Long, then enters 2–3 seconds later from Long side. Short player clears Short corner and Big Box; Long player takes far Long boxes and Goose. Mid player assists via CT Spawn as third entry.
▸ B Site Retake: CT Spawn players rotate through B Mid (Scaffold/Window) and enter from B Doors — not through Tunnels (too slow, single-file). Flash into B Site from B Doors before entering. Molotov B platform or Short post-plant position. Long A player swings through CT as a delayed third entry. Never send more than 2 CTs through Tunnels.

#### Adapting to T Reads
▸ If Ts are fast rushing B Tunnels (5-man or 3-man rush):
- B anchor retreats to Back Plat immediately — do NOT fight in Tunnels. Let Ts exit Tunnels and take the B Site crossfire.
- IGL rotates to B Doors. Mid player provides a third angle from Window.
▸ If Ts are slow-defaulting with Long + Mid setup:
- AWP takes an aggressive early Car peek to gather information. Mid player pushes Catwalk self-boost (new CS2 mechanic — solo boost now possible) to gather T Spawn-side info.
- Stack A if information confirms heavy Long presence — B anchor holds solo while the rest collapse A.
▸ If Ts run a Mid-split B execute (1 via Catwalk/Short, 2 via Tunnels):
- Mid player must delay Catwalk with flash + peek — buying time for B anchor to hold until rotation arrives.
- AWPer drops from Car to A Short to cover the Short flank angle while B anchor holds.

#### Pro-Level Nuances
▸ AWP on Car is mandatory: Every top pro team runs at least one AWP on Car every round — it is the single highest-value defensive position in CS2. Never move the AWP off Car at round start without a clear plan.
▸ HE into B Tunnels every round: Pro teams almost always invest an HE into Upper Tunnels to chip Ts; amateur CTs skip this and get rushed with full HP.
▸ Mid control = site control: A passive mid player allows Ts to split B (through B Doors) and A (through Catwalk) simultaneously with minimal coordination. Mid cannot be abandoned.
▸ Long patience: Amateur CTs over-peek Long and die to coordinated pushes. Pro CTs use Car cover and bait Ts into poor positions before showing themselves.
▸ Rotation discipline: Dust2's A-to-B rotation takes 7–9 seconds — pro CTs never rotate both A players at once; one goes, one stays until bomb plant is confirmed.
▸ 2024 Catwalk self-boost: A solo CT can now boost to Catwalk without a partner — use this to gather T Spawn-side information; this is a new information play unavailable before the CS2 update.
