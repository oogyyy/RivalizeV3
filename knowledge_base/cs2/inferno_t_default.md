# Inferno — T-Side Default

**Map overview (T-perspective):** Inferno is a slow, utility-heavy map. T-side wins by either controlling Banana to pressure B, or using Apartments to apply A-side pressure. Splitting both lanes simultaneously is the highest-pressure strategy. The CT AWP at Second Mid is the most dangerous defensive tool — neutralizing it is priority one.

---

## Starting Positions

| Role | Callout | Opening Job |
|---|---|---|
| AWPer | T-Spawn → Banana | Open Banana duel vs CT AWP; establish Banana control |
| Entry | T-Spawn → Apartments | Walk Apartments for A-Main info and CT aggression check |
| Support | T-Spawn → Banana (with AWPer) | Throw Banana molotov; flash for AWPer's duel |
| Lurker | T-Spawn → Apartments (with Entry) | Trail Entry; cut off CT rotation via Library or Arch |
| IGL | T-Spawn → Banana OR stays T-Spawn | Calls based on Banana vs Apartments pressure reports |

**Default split: 3 Banana, 2 Apartments**

---

## Default Phase (1:40 → 1:00)

AWPer and Support create Banana pressure while Entry and Lurker apply soft Apartments pressure to gather A-side CT positions. The key information objective is: how many CTs are playing Banana vs. how many are anchoring A?

**Utility in this phase:**
- **Support** throws molotov at **Top of Banana** (First Banana) at 1:52 to clear CT aggression — [▶ Watch lineup](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=cs2+inferno+molotov+t+spawn+to+top+banana)

## Economy Considerations

- **Pistol T (Round 1):** 3 rush Banana (AWPer, Entry, Support); 2 slow-play Apartments. Molotov first corner on Banana with eco pistol round.
- **Anti-Eco:** Force a B rush — B is fastest for anti-eco with 4 players.
- **Force Buy:** Coordinated rush Banana with AKs; skip utility, force Second Banana at 1:00 and execute fast.
