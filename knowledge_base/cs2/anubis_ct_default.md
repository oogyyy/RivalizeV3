# CT-Side Default for de_anubis

#### Introduction
Anubis is the most T-sided map in the active pool (~56–57% T win rate historically) — CT philosophy must compensate through early aggression and mid denial. Passivity loses on Anubis. CTs must contest the Bridge / Connector fight early, use elevated Heaven positions on both sites, and never let Ts walk mid or B Ruins unopposed. The January 2026 update modified the Water-to-Bridge control path — all pre-2026 mid utility lineups should be treated as potentially obsolete.

#### Opening Utility Usage (0-5s)
▸ [B Anchor 1]: Throw B Canal / Water molotov from B Site area landing at Canal entry — delays T Water passage and prevents early B pressure via Canal route ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [A Anchor]: Flash A Main entry from CT A Side — blind-flash toward the A Main approach to punish any immediate T A rush ▶ Watch lineup.
▸ [AWPer/Mid]: Throw Mid / Green Door smoke from CT direction landing at Green Door exit or E-Box at mid — cuts off T early mid peeking; denies the most dangerous T sightline from mid ▶ Watch lineup.

#### Default Positions
▸ [AWPer]: Play A Heaven or Bridge — the most powerful position on the map post-January 2026; overlooks both mid and A Connector approach; takes an aggressive early Bridge peek to catch Ts crossing before they set utility.
▸ [A Anchor]: Hold A Site (behind column or site cover) — watches A Main entry and A Short; never leaves the site unanchored.
▸ [Mid Player / Support]: Hold Connector or E-Box area — watches Green Door, E-Box, and Bridge; contests mid actively; passive mid on Anubis allows Ts to dictate the entire round.
▸ [B Anchor 1]: Hold Pillar (B Site) — one of the strongest B positions; the Pillar off-angle is hard to flash and has multiple escape routes; plays an unexpected side of Pillar to catch T demo-prepared players.
▸ [IGL / B Anchor 2]: Hold B Heaven or Water (Canal) — watches B Ruins approach and Water exit; B Heaven gives a complete overview of B Site; never abandon B Heaven without trading a kill first.

#### Rotation Rules
▸ If Bridge / mid fight won by Ts: full CT collapse — A Heaven player falls back toward A Site; IGL repositions to CT; both sites play passive; do not try to contest mid after losing Bridge.
▸ If A Connector smoke heard (A execute likely): AWP from Heaven re-angles toward A Main; mid player falls back to CT; A anchor tightens on site.
▸ If Water footsteps heard (multiple Ts — B execute likely): B Heaven player drops to site immediately; IGL calls A player to begin rotation via CT.
▸ If bomb planted A: B players rotate via CT through Canal (shorter than it appears); smoke A Short to cut T flank on rotation.
▸ If bomb planted B: A players rotate through Connector toward B CT; A anchor stays to prevent T split from A side.

#### Crossfire Setups
▸ A Heaven + A Short (A Site): Heaven player watches Connector / A left approach from above; A Short player watches the right entry from ground level. T pushing A Main must clear both the aerial Heaven angle and the Short ground angle — the height difference makes this extremely difficult without specific utility.
▸ Pillar + B Heaven (B Site): Pillar player holds central B entry from ground; Heaven player holds from above on the opposite side. T coming through B Ruins must deal with a ground angle and an aerial angle simultaneously; one flash cannot blind both.
▸ Bridge + Connector (Mid): CT mid player holds from E-Box / Connector; AWPer holds from Bridge or Heaven. Any T crossing mid is exposed to two very different engagement ranges simultaneously.

#### Retake Coordination
▸ A Site Retake: A Heaven player (if alive) holds until team is ready — does NOT chase into a 1vX. CT Spawn player pushes through A Short — throws flash into site before entry. Smoke A Main exit from CT Connector to block T post-plant positions. Molotov A Heaven ledge (common T post-plant position) if Ts are known to hold there. A Short entry player clears left; CT Spawn player clears right — two-angle clear.
▸ B Site Retake: IGL advances through Canal (Water) — smoke Gate before pushing out into B Site; Gate without smoke gives Ts a free kill every retake. B Heaven player drops down with a flash timed to IGL's Canal entry. Second CT enters from B mid via the Connector corridor. Molotov B Pillar corner (common post-plant spot). Gate smoke on B retake is non-negotiable — never skip it.

#### Adapting to T Reads
▸ If Ts are fast rushing B Main with no utility:
- B anchors hold deep (Pillar and B Heaven) — do not aggress B Main corridor; use the B Site crossfire instead.
- IGL rotates B immediately from B Heaven position without waiting for confirmation.
▸ If Ts are running a slow default with heavy B Main utility:
- Mid player times the B Main smoke and peeks from Connector / E-Box after smoke lands to catch Ts in the corridor mid-smoke.
- Consider re-aggressing B Main entry before smokes expire — Ts are stationary during utility.
▸ If Ts are running the signature B 3+2 Canal split:
- B Heaven player must stay high and NOT be baited into dropping early — the Canal players arrive after the B Main entry; holding Heaven denies the split angle.
- IGL holds Gate with smoke to block Canal players from freely accessing B Site; Gate smoke delays the entire Canal rotation.

#### Pro-Level Nuances
▸ Mid is the whole map: Unlike most CS2 maps where mid is important, on Anubis it is essentially the entire round. Pro CTs invest aggressively in mid fights — passive mid = passive death.
▸ T-sided map demands CT aggression: Because Anubis is heavily T-favored, pro CTs deliberately contest early (peeks + flashes) to disrupt T setups. Passive CT play on Anubis concedes the map.
▸ January 2026 geometry changes: The Water-to-Bridge path was altered to reduce T mid dominance. Any pre-2026 utility lineups for mid are potentially obsolete — rebuild from scratch.
▸ Pillar off-angle: The Pillar position looks exposed but is one of the strongest 1vX delay positions on B because it is hard to flash effectively and has multiple escape angles.
▸ B Heaven non-negotiable: Pro CTs never abandon B Heaven without trading a kill first. Heaven gives the most complete B Site overview of any position — losing it for free collapses B.
▸ Gate smoke on B retake: Pro teams always smoke Gate before any B retake entry. Charging Gate without a smoke gives the defending T a free kill every single time.
